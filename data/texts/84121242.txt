The possibility of excelling and winning is the main motivation in life for many peole. In the modern world, there is competition everywhere: at work, at school, on the road, in the gym, and more. We learn how to get better and succeed by contrasting ourselves with one another and competing against one another. Contrarily, cooperation has a lot of advantages that can help us in practically every part of life, including work, education, and daily life. Employees who cooperate with one another are more likely to boost output and innovate, frequently in ways that are more advanced than those permitted by competition.

l'd like to clarify what the two phrases mean before delving more into the subject. Competition: "to compare" means to make an effort to outdo or surpass rivals who are making an effort to acheive the same goal. Contrarily, cooperation is defined as "working together for a common goal; sharing an activity for mutual gain. 

We are debating whether cooperation or competition is a better strategy for obtaining excellence; from my perspective, cooperation is the better strategy for reaching excellence for a number of reasons. But this raises the question: What constitutes
excellence? Yes, there are "excellent qualities in great degree, "but what is "good" frequently depends on one's perspective. One man's garbage is another man's treasure, therefore greatness for one individual might not equal excellence for another.
