Materialism in todays society has been on a rise year after year. Stores pushing holiday themed plastic products has increased the purchasing of short term use products. With the increase of supply and marketing I assume materialism will continue to rise to unsustainable heights. Increasing plastic waste and clutter.

How many of us have a home over run with clutter due to impulse buying? Materialism at its core has caused many of us to be overwelmed in our own home. Rather than having a practical home decor we've turned to making our homes resemble a page from a magazine. Buying items that serve no other purpose than to be aesthetically pleasing. Of couse this is all fine, but where do we draw the line. I can't tell you how many times I've realized my home is over run with products I've bought on impulse that I can't justify taking up space in my home.

So should we criticize modern society for being materialistic? In my opinon that is the very thing we should be doing. While it is perfectly fine to indulge at times, we need to be more conscience of our acquisition of possessions.
