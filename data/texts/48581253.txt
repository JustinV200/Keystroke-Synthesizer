Evolutionary theorists, like all scientists, are a disagreeable sort. They disagree on the timing of the diaspora from Africa, the exsistance of 'the missing link' and the reason our great-great grand-primates left the trees. 

Perhaps in a unified fit of rebellion against Father Darwin, however, they agree that our species would not exsist had we attempted  an extraordinary task like evolution while simultaneously stabbing each other in the back with ruthless ambition.

We achieved OURSELVES through cooperation, then, having done so (and presumably taking a break to congratulate ourselves for a job well done), went on to achieve everything else that we now credit to mankind.

Unfortunately, we eventually got around to inventing concepts like 'competition'. 

You see, unlike the incidental and unavoidable competition for resources that's necessary for survival,  INTENTIONAL competition is not part of our hardwiring. The family groups and tribes on the road to homosapien that harbored the competitive streak wouldn't have lasted long in the harsh realities of prehistory. 
Any member of the group not acting in the best interest of the whole would have quickly found themselves ejected from the group (and with far fewer opportunities to pass on their abberent genetic material.)
