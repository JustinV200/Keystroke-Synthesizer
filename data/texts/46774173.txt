Everyday, people are inundated with hundreds, if not thousands of images a day.  Our ability to see the world, and the connections that we make with what we see is an important evolutionary aspect that makes us different from other species.  We have this capacity to feel emotions, that often come to be the driving force of our actions, even at times 'overriding' our natural instinct to survive.  Companies, have utilized marketing campaigns to target our emotional responses, in hopes of harnessing them towards whatever product of idea that is being sold or promoted.  So often, humans become so enamored with the aesthetics of things, that they become blind or possibly just ignorant to the deeper meaning that may lie behind an exterior.  This notion is pervasive through more than just the realm of marketing and advertisements, as often that first visual is the basis for the judgement that we make whether it be a person, a car, a house, or even a sandwich at a restaurant.  We have been trained to look at and for the objects that are most appealing, almost diminishing any further value beyond an external facade.
